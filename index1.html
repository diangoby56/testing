About the Founders of the Schoolhouse


Adapted Education Formal education adapted to the particular needs and practical goals of our students. Transferable Skection Teaching approaches and curriculum adapted to the student background and current needs. Student Involvement The Student Board members take active roles in decision making and activity planning. Salary-paid Staff The teachers receive a salary as well as other staff in key positions. Self-sustainability The program runs on a low budget, and recruits in-kind donations and partnerships. Student Payment The students pay for the courses and by doing so are partners in supporting the program. Students are not prevented from participating if unable to pay. Certificates The students recieve certificates at the end of the semester in a formal Certificate Ceremony.

The students at The Schoolhouse are men and women ages 18-40, refugees, asylum seekers or migrants, from countries which they fled or left for reasons of life danger, persecution, oppression, poverty, or lack of freedom, or equal opportunity. The majority of our students are from origin countries; Sudan and Eritrea. We also have students from: Nigeria, Ivory Coast, Bolivia, Venezuela and more. Their educational background varies, ranging from those whom have never been to school before, to University graduates. There are quite a few autodeducts. Many left their countries unexpectedly and have since been coping with survival challenges, physical or mental illness without proper care, instability and the unknown, loneliness, cultural acclimation difficulties, and above all, great hopes and dreams for the future. Our students have mostly survived against all odds, and are deeply motivated towards life. Education is viewed as a significant tool for empowerment, independence, employment opportunity, and self- development, all which will equip them with greater abilities for making an impact and creating a better future, for themselves, the community around them, or back in their country of origin.

Schoolhouse

The Schoolhouse promotes adult education for refugees, asylum seekers and marginalized migrant populations in Israel.

Our ultimate goal is to see our students bringing advancements to society around them, developing careers or businesses, working in higher salary work places, becoming influential individuals within their communities or back in their countries of origin, or simply developing into the people they want to be, living the life they want to lead.

OUR STORY
Between 2007-2011, as Africans from Sudan and Eritrea streamed into Israel's Southern border seeking asylum and hopes for a better life, the Israeli government remained unprepared as to how to approach this new migration challenge. The struggles on behalf of human rights organizations focused on survival needs, psycho-social attention, policy change, rights, medical treatment and more. Yet many voices from within the African asylum seeker population were speaking of education. There was a huge motivation to create a better future for themselves and their communities and education was viewed as a significant tool for being able to impact a change. The Schoolhouse educational model was created to develop adult education and skills adapted to the particular needs of this community. In January 2012, the first Schoolhouse English class began, together with partner organizations HIAS and Windows for Peace.
